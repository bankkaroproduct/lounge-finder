# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Dual audience: (1) Frequent Indian flyers with premium credit cards (HDFC Infinia, Axis Magnus, Amex Platinum) who travel 10+ times per year and know their lounge rights; (2) Occasional travelers who have a mid-tier card and want to know whether they qualify before arriving. Both have the same job: get to the airport and walk into the right lounge without confusion or wasted time at the terminal.

## Product Purpose

LoungeHopper maps which Indian airport lounges a set of credit cards unlocks. Users save their wallet once; the app remembers it. At any airport, before or during travel, they get a precise list of lounges they can walk into with cards they already own. Success = zero uncertainty at the terminal.

## Brand Personality

Earned access. Precise and confident. Never generic.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard: repeated card grids, icon + heading + body templates, blue-grey utility feel.
- OTA chaos: MakeMyTrip-style discount-badge clutter, multi-column filter panels, noise.
- Corporate bank portal: institutional, clunky, form-heavy.

## Design Principles

1. The answer first — show the lounge list without hunting; clarity above all.
2. Save once, check forever — the wallet is a first-class feature, not just another input.
3. Earned confidence — the design belongs in a business class lounge, not a ticketing queue.
4. One strong signal per card — never let five competing labels cancel each other out.
5. Confirmed, not guessed — when access rules are uncertain, surface it honestly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Full keyboard navigation. prefers-reduced-motion supported: all transitions collapse to near-instant for users who opt out.
